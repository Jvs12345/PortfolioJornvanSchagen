Case 01 / Company details anonymised

Intralogistics Flow Optimization & Material Handling Transformation

Period: 2022 project phase | My contribution: Individual ownership of flow analysis, AGV vs tugger evaluation, and milk run design logic.

Functional intralogistics redesign for high-frequency internal transport, built around lower investment intensity and better flow logic than the AGV alternative.

The challenge

The question was whether automated guided vehicles were the right answer for repetitive, high-frequency internal movement. The environment was busy, dense, and sensitive to route design, so a technology-led answer risked solving the wrong problem.

  • High flow intensity across multiple internal zones
  • Need for reliable replenishment rhythm rather than isolated trips
  • Pressure to choose a scalable system without overinvesting

The decision logic

I argued for a tugger train milk run system instead of AGVs. The key was synchronised route design: one train, multiple deliveries, timed loops, and service frequency weighted by ABC demand levels so high-movement zones received more frequent stops.

  • Lean Six Sigma framing instead of technology hype
  • ABC weighting used to determine route frequency and stop priority
  • Focus on process integrity, repeatability, and throughput

The business result

The proposal showed that a simpler, more synchronised transport logic could reduce projected equipment investment versus the AGV alternative while maintaining strong flow performance. It also reduced waste by consolidating shipments into planned train runs and leveling workload more effectively.

  • Lower projected equipment investment than the AGV proposal
  • Stronger workload leveling through timed milk runs
  • High-frequency zones served through ABC-weighted route logic
  • Clear demonstration that smart flow can outperform expensive automation

Case 02 / Reverse logistics prototype

Camera-Assisted Detection for Reverse Logistics Decisions

Period: 2024 project sprint | My contribution: Individual design and build of the reverse-logistics decision prototype and dashboard logic.

Functional reverse-logistics prototype using camera-assisted inspection to improve return classification and downstream handling.

The intake design

Returns often slow down because intake quality is inconsistent. This prototype focuses on creating a structured intake point where products are captured, assessed, and prepared for a clearer next-step decision.

  • Designed around reverse logistics rather than standard outbound flow
  • Visual consistency at intake to support faster triage
  • Improves decision quality before items enter the wrong stream

The inspection layer

The prototype uses camera-supported detection to flag packaging damage, visible defects, and confidence levels. The purpose is not to remove human judgement, but to support faster and more consistent first-line assessment.

  • Condition signals made visible in one inspection view
  • Supports prioritisation and consistency in return handling
  • Combines operational practicality with digital decision support

The decision outcome

Once scored, returns can be routed toward resale, refurbish, further inspection, or recycle. The value of the prototype lies in separating high-potential items earlier and reducing delay in the overall reverse flow.

  • Faster separation of value-recovery opportunities
  • More structured reverse logistics process design
  • Clearer handoff between inspection and disposition

Case 03 / Site details simplified

Warehouse Digital Twin for Route Visibility and Slotting Scenarios

Period: June 2025 | My contribution: Individual build of the digital twin workflow, scenario framing, and stakeholder walkthrough setup.

Interactive routing prototype designed to visualize congestion, slotting scenarios, and stakeholder walkthroughs inside a functional warehouse environment.

The operational tool

Warehouse decisions often stay hidden in tables. This project translated them into a visual environment where routes, locations, and bottlenecks could be understood spatially.

  • Route visibility across a warehouse layout
  • More intuitive understanding of picking movement
  • Useful for explaining scenario differences to stakeholders
  • Interactive POV mode so users can walk the floor virtually

The analysis logic

The prototype was supported by data work around slotting, congestion, and process patterns so the interface was not just visual, but tied to operational logic and measurable outcomes.

  • Scenario comparison for different slotting approaches
  • Support for KPI-oriented performance review
  • Stronger bridge between analysis and communication
  • Roughly 4,500 lines across the main prototype files, including a custom-coded 3D warehouse environment

Why it matters

The real value was making warehouse design conversations easier. A digital twin style tool helps teams discuss trade-offs faster, especially when routes, density, and flow complexity are hard to explain in static spreadsheets alone.

  • Better discussion around layout and movement
  • More accessible communication of technical analysis
  • Clearer operational storytelling for decision makers
  • WASD-enabled warehouse POV for immersive stakeholder review
  • Well suited for live walkthroughs or short stakeholder screen recordings

Case 04 / WMS data checking tool

WMS Table Scraper and Leakage Checker

Period: 2025 internal tooling phase | My contribution: Individual development of the WMS scrape, Excel comparison, and mismatch detection flow.

A small internal tool that scraped WMS tables, compared them with Excel exports, and surfaced mismatches where the WMS showed errors but the ERP did not.

The problem

The warehouse system was showing errors that were not clearly reflected in the ERP output. That made it difficult to see where data mismatches were happening and where leakage could hide inside routine transactions.

  • WMS and ERP were not aligned
  • Manual checking took too much time
  • Needed a faster way to isolate mismatches

The checker

I built a small web scraper that pulled structured tables from the WMS, lined them up against an Excel export, and flagged the records that did not match cleanly across both views.

  • Scraped WMS tables into a repeatable checking flow
  • Compared live warehouse records against export data
  • Turned hidden mismatches into a clear exception list

Why it mattered

The tool made it easier to spot leakage between systems, reduce manual hunting, and give both operations and finance a clearer basis for follow-up.

  • Made mismatches visible faster
  • Reduced manual comparison work
  • Linked operational errors to data integrity checks

Case 06 / Inventory control dashboard

Cycle Counting and Inventory Accuracy Monitoring

Period: 2024-2025 internship phase | My contribution: Individual cycle-count optimization and Power BI dashboard development for staff performance and inventory control.

Power BI-based cycle counting dashboard built to improve inventory accuracy, show staff performance, and encourage more proactive counting on the warehouse floor.

The problem

The cycle counting process needed more structure and visibility. Counting had to fit around daily operations, discrepancies needed faster follow-up, and staff needed a clearer view of progress and performance to keep counting proactive instead of reactive.

  • Inventory differences were hard to track consistently
  • Counting activity needed to happen more often without disruption
  • Recently mutated articles should be excluded for a set window to avoid operational disturbance
  • Supervisors and staff needed visibility on count performance

The dashboard

I built a Power BI dashboard around the new cycle counting setup so count completion, discrepancy trends, and staff performance could be reviewed in one place. A key design decision was to avoid counting any article within a defined time window after a mutation, so cycle counts would not clash with active warehouse movements or create avoidable disruption.

  • Count progress and discrepancy patterns brought into one view
  • Mutation-based exclusion window protected the operation during active stock changes
  • Staff could see their own performance and counting contribution
  • Useful for daily control, meetings, and prioritising follow-up

Why it mattered

The project helped turn cycle counting into an active management tool. It supported more frequent counts, reduced discrepancies in testing, and gave warehouse staff an incentive to count proactively because performance became visible.

  • More counts per day without disrupting operations
  • Count timing was aligned with warehouse flow instead of interrupting live mutations
  • Better visibility on inventory accuracy and count discipline
  • Clearer steering for both supervisors and warehouse staff
